Constitutional amendments (5/13):

As was discussed in the General Body meeting, the Coordinating Committee recommended adopting the amendments to the constitution proposed by Constitutional Reform Working Group. Members voted through the Helios online voting platform. The vote was open from 5:30pm on Wednesday, May 10th and closed at 5:30pm on Saturday, May 13th. 

There were 455 total voters, and the results were as follows:

Question #1
Should GET-UP adopt the ‘No Harm Clause’ proposed by the Constitutional Reform Working Group?

Yes 449
No 6

Question #2
Should GET-UP adopt the ‘Strike Clause’ proposed by the Constitutional Reform Working Group?

Yes 434
No 20

Question #3
Should GET-UP adopt the three amendments titled “Elections and Standing Committees” proposed by the Constitutional Reform Working Group?

Yes 442
No 9

Question #4
Should GET-UP adopt the five amendments titled “Minor Changes” proposed by the Constitutional Reform Working Group?

Yes 441
No 12

 

Proposed constitutional amendments:

 

1. No Harm Clause

No graduate student worker shall have their stipend and benefits reduced as a consequence of that individual’s assignment to a position covered by any agreement ratified by the membership of GET-UP.

The aim of our campaign is to improve the material conditions of graduate students via collective power. We take grads’ concerns about their circumstances very seriously. We are proposing to incorporate a ‘no harm clause’ into our constitution, which will ensure that a contract will not lead to a real-wage decrease or loss of benefits for any members. The purpose of this clause is to ensure that no graduate student employee receiving a fellowship stipend shall have that stipend reduced as a consequence of holding a position that is covered by our union contract. It is a guarantee to members that they will not lose out by supporting our union.

 

2. Strike Clause

Pursuant to Subsection B2, 4. of “Section B: Decision Making,” the decision to strike will require a supportive supermajority of 66% of the total membership, and a meeting of the general body to discuss the strike must be called prior to the referendum. Graduate student workers will be permitted to cross a picket in order to prevent the loss of academic work (e.g. to preserve a time-sensitive scientific experiment).

Currently, strikes are defined in the constitution as a “substantive decision,” because they would impact upon the long-term collective bargaining position of GET-UP. Substantive decisions are subject to a referendum, which is decided by a simple majority when ⅓ of members have voted. The constitution working group proposes that the decision to strike require a higher bar: rather than a quorum, 66% of total membership must support a strike for the ballot to take effect. Setting the bar at 66% of membership will ensure that no single school or department will be able to dominate the ballot (e.g. SAS comprises approximately 44% of the unit, BGS approximately 23%, SEAS approximately 15%, Wharton approximately 6%, and GSE approximately 4%). That a strike would only proceed with a coalition of voters from multiple schools will also indicate that the strike has enough support to be effective. The second line of the clause recognizes that graduate student workers in STEM fields may need to continue or protect their work during a strike, and ensures that they will not be penalized for doing so.

 

3. Elections and Standing Committees

“Article II: Union Governance,” “Section B: The Standing Committees,” 2 is hereby repealed. It shall now read:

“Elections will be held each April to elect members to standing committees of the following academic year. The first set of elections will be held when GET-UP wins a recognition election. Until such initial elections are held, the day to day operational responsibilities of GET-UP will be the responsibility of a voluntary Coordinating Committee with roles assigned on an ad hoc basis. Upon the removal or replacement of an officer or the creation of a new position, an interim election shall be held to appoint an individual with a term running until the next April election.

The original constitution stipulated that elections for standing committees would take place in the fall of 2016. Due to the rate of growth of GET-UP and the need to incorporate as many new members as possible, the standing committees continued to meet on an ad-hoc basis for the remainder of the academic year. The text in blue will bring the constitution into line with that decision, and provide for the election of members to standing committees after GET-UP has won a recognition election. At that point, everyone in the university will have heard about and had to the opportunity to join GET-UP.

 

Pursuant to “Article V: Elections,” the Election Committee will also be considered a standing committee.

The Election Committee was intended to be a standing committee but is not currently listed as such. This amendment corrects that oversight.

 

The “Unity Committee” may rename itself as it sees appropriate.

Some members of the Unity Committee have expressed dissatisfaction with the name of the committee. The Unity Committee should get to call itself whatever it wants, and have the result of its deliberations recorded formally in the constitution.

 

4. Minor Changes

There are a number of minor textual changes that have either been requested by members of the Penn community or are needed to bring the constitution into line with current practice:

 

Membership Card:

Appendix A is hereby repealed. The following membership card will be used instead:

Since GET-UP went public, we’ve been using a new membership card, both parts of which need to be signed and dated in order to join. This amendment will install that card in the constitution. Changes to the membership card statement would mean that individuals who signed the old membership cards will no longer be considered active members under the new revisions.

Preamble:

We, the graduate academic workers of the University of Pennsylvania, recognize ourselves as a community of scholars, teachers, and researchers. Despite our differences across disciplines, programs, and departments, we all ultimately labor under common working conditions, face similar challenges, and share related grievances. We therefore aim to develop our collective power to improve these working conditions, enrich our academic experience, and transform the university into a more democratic workplace for the benefit of the wider community, including undergraduates, standing and non-standing faculty, staff, and the City of Philadelphia. We also aim to improve the hiring, acceptance, and retention of academic workers from historically underrepresented groups, cognizant that these selection procedures and associated working conditions determine the diversity of the labor force at the University of Pennsylvania. Recognizing that such change can never occur nor be maintained without solidarity, sustained organization, and collective action, we, the graduate academic workers of the University of Pennsylvania, organize ourselves into a Union under the name of Graduate Employees Together at the University of Pennsylvania (GET-UP) and give ourselves the following Constitution:

The original language distinguished between non-standing faculty and adjuncts, where we believe that adjuncts are non-standing faculty. The new language in blue corrects this misapprehension and clarifies the nature of the wider community.

 

Employed Peoples

“Article I: Membership,” “Section A: Eligibility,” 1.b. is hereby repealed.

“Section A: Eligibility,” 1.b technically disenfranchises some members of the Graduate School of Education, who work for the university while pursuing their degree.

 

Membership Cards

Article I: Membership,” “Section B: Membership Procedure,” 1.a will now read: “Each card must be printed on paper, filled out, and physically signed in ink.” All digital signatures received thus far are valid. Digital signatures received in the future will be accepted.

GET-UP has been accepting digital signatures to accommodate those away from campus. This amendment brings the constitution into line with that practice.

 

Article I: Membership,” “Section B: Membership Procedure,” 2 will now read: “The signed card must be returned to the Coordinating Committee, which will be filed in the Union archive.”

GET-UP currently files signed membership cards in the union archive and provided copies on request. This amendment brings the membership procedure into line with current practice.
